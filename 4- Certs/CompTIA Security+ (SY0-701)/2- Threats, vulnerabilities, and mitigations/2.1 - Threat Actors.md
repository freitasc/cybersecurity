## What is it?

- Entities responsible for events that have impact on the safety of another entity
	- Also called a malicious actor or adversary
	- It is useful to categorize the motivation
		- Why is this attack happening?
		- Is this directed or random?
		- Who are the actors?
		- Are they a criminal group? A country's hacker team? A competitor?
## Additional Information

- Attributes of the threat actors
	- Internal
		- The attacker is inside the house
		- A dissatisfied employee contacted by criminals
		- A past employee with working credentials
		- A contracted third-party company in bed with competitors
	- External
		- The attacker is outside the house
		- Criminal organizations
		- Countries hacker teams
		- A competitor of the company trying to disrupt business
	- Resources / funding
		- Money
		- Computing power (specially for DDOS)
		- Manpower
	- Level of sophistication
		- Script Kiddies vs Bleeding Edge technology
		- The attacker can be anything between a doofus and the CIA with no-click zero-day exploits
		- Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs) are famous for being highly sophisticated hacker groups, sometimes with governmental ties

- Motivations of threat actors
	- Data exfiltration
	- Espionage
	- Service disruption
	- Blackmail
	- Financial gain
	- Philosophical / Political / Ethical beliefs
	- Revenge
	- Disruption / Chaos for the sake of it
	- War / Counter Intel / Intelligence Agencies 


## Examples

- Nation states
	- ==External entity==
	- Motivation: Data Exfiltration, ==Political==, War
	- Resources: ==Almost infinite== money, computing power and manpower
	- Sophistication: APTs, ==the best of the best hackers==, years of planning
	- The US and Israel destroyed 1000 nuclear centrifuges using the Stuxnet worm, completely built in secret and designed to destroy this targets

- Unskilled / Script Kiddies
	- ==External entity== but rarely a insider threat aswell
	- Motivation: ==The hunt itself==, data exfiltration, disruption, financial gain and sometimes philosophical reasons
	- Resources: ==Very limited==, self-hosted servers, own computer
	- Sophistication: ==No sophistication==, code scraped online from github or bought in the darknet, standard open source tools like metasploit

- Hacktivist
	- ==External entity==, sometimes internal as a double-agent
	- Motivation: Manly ==philosophical reasons==... but also data exfiltration, disruption and sometimes financial gain for funding the next attack
	- Resources: ==Unknown==, could be script kiddies or could be APTs or governments
	- Sophistication: ==Very specific== hacks targeting specific types of technology and orgs

- Organized crime
	- ==External entity==, sometimes can reach employees or ex-employees
	- Motivation: ==Financial gain== is top priority
	- Resources: ==Lots and lots of money== from past crimes, could be APTs
	- Sophistication: ==Very sophisticated==, the best you can buy with money, a lot of dark web connections, sometimes correlated to dark web marketplaces

- Shadow IT
	- ==Insider threat==, working around the internal IT
	- ==SOMETIMES ITS JUST DUMB EMPLOYEES NOT FOLLOWING PROCEDURES==
	- Build their own infra inside the organization's infra
	- Motivation: Philosophical or Ethical Beliefs, ==revenge against current or past employees==
	- Resources: All or most of ==the organization's own resources== as they build roadblocks inside the organization's infrastructure
	- Sophistication: ==Medium sophistication== as this are experienced IT employees and security experts, they know all the companies procedures and playbooks... but are not full time criminals nor APTs
