## What is it?

- How to classify and protect different data
## Additional Information

- Regulated
	- Managed by a third-party
	- Government laws and statutes
- Trade Secret
	- An organization's secrets
	- Often unique to an organization
- Intellectual property
	- May be public visible
	- Copyright and trademark restrictions
	- Watermarked images
- Legal information
	- Court records and documents, judge and attorney info
	- PII and other sensitive details
	- Usually stored in many different systems depending on how sensitive it is
- Financial information -> PCI DSS
	- Internal company financial details
	- Customer financials
	- Payment records
	- Credit card data, bank records, etc.

- Data types
	- Human-readable
		- Plaintext usually
	- Non-human readable
		- Barcodes, images, encoded or hashed data
	- Some formats are hybrids
		- CSV, XML, JSON etc

- Data classifying
	- Not all data has the same level of categorization
		- License tag numbers vs health records
	- Different levels require different security and handling
		- Additional permissions
		- A different process to view
		- Restricted network access
	- Proprietary
		- Data that is property of an organization
		- Maybe trade secrets
	- PII - Personally identifiable information
		- Data that can be used to identify an individual
		- Name, date of birth, mother's name, biometric info
	- PHI - Protected Health information
		- Health information associated with an individual
		- Health status, health care records and more

- Examples of data classification
	- Sensitive
		- Intellectual property, PII, PHI
	- Confidential
		- Very sensitive, each data must be approved to view
	- Public / Unclassified
		- No restrictions
	- Private / Classified / Restricted
		- Restricted access, may require an NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
	- Critical
		- Data should always be accessible
