## What is it?

- How to classify and protect different data
## Additional Information

- Regulated
	- Managed by a third-party
	- Government laws and statutes
- Trade Secret
	- An organization's secrets
	- Often unique to an organization
- Intellectual property
	- May be public visible
	- Copyright and trademark restrictions
	- Watermarked images
- Legal information
	- Court records and documents, judge and attorney info
	- PII and other sensitive details
	- Usually stored in many different systems depending on how sensitive it is
- Financial information -> PCI DSS
	- Internal company financial details
	- Customer financials
	- Payment records
	- Credit card data, bank records, etc.

- Data types
	- Human-readable
		- Plaintext usually
	- Non-human readable
		- Barcodes, images, encoded or hashed data
	- Some formats are hybrids
		- CSV, XML, JSON etc

- Data classifying
	- Not all data has the same level of categorization
		- License tag numbers vs health records
	- Different levels require different security and handling
		- Additional permissions
		- A different process to view
		- Restricted network access
	- Proprietary
		- Data that is property of an organization
		- Maybe trade secrets
	- PII - Personally identifiable information
		- Data that can be used to identify an individual
		- Name, date of birth, mother's name, biometric info
	- PHI - Protected Health information
		- Health information associated with an individual
		- Health status, health care records and more

- Examples of data classification
	- Corporate / Private Classifications
		- Public
			- No restrictions, anyone can see
			- ==Company contact info, everything displayed on their web pages==
		- Sensitive
			- Some restriction, no need for a NDA but shouldn't be public for strategic reasons
			- Unauthorized access could lead to privacy violations or misuse
			- ==Phone numbers, emails, and other contact info (low risk PII)==
		- Private
			- Restricted access, may require an NDA (Non-Disclosure Agreement)
			- Leakage could lead to serious legal problems
			- ==Employee PII, pentesting results, risk assessments==
		- Confidential / Proprietary
			- Very sensitive, each data must be approved to view
			- If it leaks, it leads to loss of competitive advantage, or severe law punishment
			- ==Commercial secrets, source-codes, social security numbers, credit card numbers==
	- Governmental / Military Classifications
		- Unclassified
			- Open to the eyes of the public
			- ==Most juridical documents that can be searched on government websites==
		- Classified
			- Very sensitive, each data must be approved to view
			- Could lead to damage to the organization
			- ==Less sensitive intelligence reports, pentesting results, training profile results==
		- Secret
			- The first level of confidentiality
			- A secret even within the organization, only for certain eyes
			- Could lead to serious damage to the organization
			- ==Military operations planning, coordinates of future attacks==
		- Top Secret
			- The highest level of confidentiality there is
			- A secret within a secret, probably won't ever leave the room
			- Can compromise national security or start a civil war
			- ==State secrets, CIA files, espionage tactics==

- Critical
	- Critical data is a kind of data that NEEDS to be available at ALL TIMES
	- Medical data of patients, sky control data for planes, nuclear power plant data
