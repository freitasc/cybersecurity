## What is it?

- How to secure things that are always connected

## Additional Information

- Site surveys
	- Determine existing wireless landscape
	- Identify existing access points
	- Work around existing frequencies
		- Layout and plan for interference
	- Plan for ongoing surveys
		- Things will certainly change
	- Heat Maps 
		- Identify wireless signal strengths
- Wireless survey tools
	- Signal coverage test
	- Check for potential interference
	- Most OSs have built-in tools
	- 3rd party tools are available
		- NetSpot for example
	- Spectrum analyzer
- Mobile Device Management (MDM)
	- Centralized management of mobile devices
	- Set policies on apps, data, camera etc
		- The entire device or a "partition"
	- Manage access control
		- Force screen locks and PINs every X minutes
- BYOD
	- Bring your own device
	- Employee owns the device
	- Difficult to secure
- COPE
	- Corporate owned, personally enabled
	- Company buys the devicec
	- Used as both corporate an personal device
	- Organization keeps full control of the device
	- Information is protected using corporate policies
- CYOD
	- Choose your own device
	- Similar to COPE, but withe the user's choice of device
- Cellular networks
	- Mobile devices are separated into "cells"
	- Antenna coverage a cell with certain frequencies
	- Which brings a series of security concerns
		- Traffic monitoring
		- Location tracking
		- Worldwide access to a mobile device
- Wi-Fi devices
	- Local network access
	- same security concerns as all other Wi-Fi devices
	- Data capture (Encrypt your data!)
		- Man-in-the-middle
		- Other On-path attacks
	- Denial of services by various means
- Bluetooth
	- Insecure as shit, turn it off (if possible)
	- High speed communication over short distances
	- PAN (Personal area network)
	- Connect our mobile devices together
	- Do not connect to unknown bluetooth devices
