## What is it?

- Ways to make agreements

## Additional Information

- SLA (Service Level Agreement)
	- Minimum terms for services provided
	- Commonly used between customers and service providers
	- ==Uptime, response time, performance metrics, etc==
- MOU (Memorandum of Understanding)
	- Both sides agree in general to the contests of the memorandum
	- Usually states common goals but not much more
	- Informal letter of intent, not a signed contract
- MOA (Memorandum of Agreement)
	- The next step above a MOU
	- Both sides conditionally agree to the objectives
	- ==Can also be a legal document, even without legal language==
- MSA (Master Service Agreement)
	- Legal contract and agreement of terms
	- A broad framework to cover later transactions
	- Future projects will be based on this agreement
	- ==Payment, protocol of deliveries (dates, how to, etc), guarantees, etc==
- WO (Work Order) / SOW (Statement of Work)
	- Specific list of items to be completed
	- Used in conjunction with the MSA
	- Details the scope of the job, location, deliverables schedule, acceptance criteria
	- Was the job done properly? Let's refer to the SOW.
	- ==Deliverables, results, quality standards==
- NDA (Non-disclosure agreement)
	- ==Confidentiality agreement between parties==
	- Protect confidential information
	- Unilateral or bilateral (or multilateral)
	- Formal contract, signatures are required
- BPA (Business partners agreement)
	- Going into business together
		- ==Always about companies that are EQUAL PARTNERS==
		- ==NEVER about service provisioning, if it is a service, it is probably either MSA ou SLA==
	- Decision-making
		- Who makes the business decisions?
		- The BPA lists specific individuals and scope
	- Prepare for contingencies
- SOP (Standard Operating Procedures)
	- ==Provide a consistent and documented process that employees can follow==
	- Through it businesses ensure that tasks are performed uniformly, reducing the risk of errors and inconsistencies that might lead to security vulnerabilities


- Clauses
	- Termination Clauses
		- ==Serious stuff==
		- Crime, breach of contract, disclosure of secrets, bad rep
		- Never about petty stuff like performance and deliverables
	- Data Sovereignty Clauses
		- How the local law sees data that is stored, transited and processed
		- ==ALWAYS about jurisdictional authority and GOVERNMENTS==
		- ==COMPLIANCE, GOVERNMENT, GEOGRAPHICAL LOCATION AND LAWS==
	- Data Handling and Protection Clauses
		- Not necessarily about countries
		- ALWAYS about how to store, transmit to third-parties or process data
		- It is often an agreement between organizations or organization and customer
