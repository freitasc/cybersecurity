Tags: [[Penetration Testing]]

## What is it?

- A simulated cyberattack on a system, network, or application to identify security vulnerabilities before they are exploited by malicious actors

## Additional Information

- Physical
	- Can you enter the building without a key?
	- Are there blind spots in the CCTV system?
	- Doors, windows, elevators etc
	- Against PCs
		-  Modify the boot process
		- Boot from other media
		- Modify or replace OS files

- Offensive Pentesting = Read Team
	- The attack generates the results
	- It is not about if you can defend, but rather what is vulnerable in the attack surface
	- The value is in the data generated by the attackers
- Defensive Pentesting = Blue Team
	- The defense generates the results
	- It is not about the attack, but how you defend against it
	- The value is in the data generated by the defenders
- Integrated Pentesting = Purple Team
	- Attack and defense work together
	- By working together, they can generate data that create better attacks and better defenses
	- The value is in the partnership and continuous improvement to both sides.
	- You get both a better understanding of the attack service
	- And a better defense against known vulnerabilities

- Working knowledge
	- Known environment (White box)
		- Full disclosure
	- Partially known environment (Grey box)
		- A mix of known and unknown
	- Unknown environment (Black box)
		- The pentester knows nothing
- Reconnaissance
	- Gathering a digital footprint
	- Understand the security posture
	- Minimize the attack area
	- Create a network map
	- Passive vs Active
		- Passive = Learning as much as you can from open sources (OSINT)
		- Active = Actively getting your hands dirty to get some intel
